## Chapter 2: Cognitive Layer — What Top Pages Believe

These are the implicit beliefs that underlie all top-ranking content. Content
that contradicts them tends to rank lower regardless of technical SEO.

**Belief 1: The reader is an experienced casual player, not a beginner.**
9/10 top pages assume the reader has played rummy before and has already
downloaded at least one app. None explain what rummy is. Vocabulary like
"cash tables", "practice mode", and "referral bonus" is used without definition.

**Belief 2: Money is the primary motivation — and that's fine.**
Top pages lead with earning potential. "Win real cash" appears in the above-the-fold
content of 10/10 pages. Responsible gaming disclaimers appear only in footers, if at all.

**Belief 3: Bonuses are the deciding factor, not gameplay quality.**
Every top page opens with a bonus comparison within the first 500 words.
"Welcome bonus", "referral bonus", and "daily bonus" are treated as the
primary differentiators between apps.

**Belief 4: Trust signals matter more than social proof.**
Top pages cite app store ratings, download counts, and "RNG-certified" claims.
User testimonials are rare — numbers and certifications carry trust.

**Belief 5: Mobile-first is assumed, not stated.**
No top page says "available on mobile". They say "iOS and Android" and move on.
Content is optimized for quick scanning, not long reading sessions.

## Chapter 3: Strategy Layer — How Content Is Structured

**Target word count**: 1500–2200 words.
- Short pages (< 1000 words) do not rank.
- Pages > 2500 words are rare and tend to be older, established domains.
- Sweet spot: 1700–1900 words with a tight structure.

**Heading structure (required)**:
1. H1: Matches or closely paraphrases the keyword
2. H2: Overview / "Why These Apps Made Our List" (trust-builder)
3. H2 × 5: One H2 per app reviewed (name + brief claim)
4. H2: Comparison Table (always present; often the most-linked section)
5. H2: How to Get Started / Download Guide
6. H2: FAQ (3–5 questions; addresses "is it legal" and "how to withdraw")

**Required structural elements**:
- Comparison table (app × feature matrix) — present in 9/10 top pages
- Numbered ranking (Top 5, Top 10) — present in 8/10 top pages
- App store rating mention — present in 10/10 top pages
- Direct download link or CTA button — present in 9/10 top pages

**E-E-A-T signals used by top pages**:
- Author byline with gaming/app expertise claimed (6/10 pages)
- "Last updated" date in article header (8/10 pages)
- RNG certification / government licensing mentions (7/10 pages)
- Specific numbers: download counts, active user counts (9/10 pages)

## Chapter 4: Content Layer — How It Is Written

### Title Formulas (fill-in-the-blank)

1. **Number + Superlative + Keyword + Year**: `Top 10 Best Rummy Apps in India for [Year]`
2. **Superlative + Range + Keyword**: `Best Rummy Apps in India: Top [N] Picks for Real Money`
3. **How-to + Benefit**: `How to Choose the Best Rummy App in India [Year Guide]`
4. **Review Anchor**: `[App Name] Review [Year]: Is It India's Best Rummy App?`
5. **Comparison**: `Best Rummy Apps in India vs. [Competitor]: Which Pays Out Most?`

### Intro Hook Patterns

**Hook Type 1 — Pain Point Lead** (most common, 6/10 pages):
```
Looking for the best rummy app in India that actually pays out? With over [N]
apps competing for your download, choosing the wrong one can cost you time and money.
We've tested [N] apps to find the ones worth your time.
```

**Hook Type 2 — Authority Lead** (3/10 pages):
```
We've spent [X] hours testing rummy apps available in India, analyzing their
bonus structures, withdrawal speeds, and game fairness. Here's what we found.
```

### Tone Descriptor

**Professional-casual with a hint of insider authority.**
- Not academic, not salesy.
- Write as a trusted friend who also happens to be an expert.
- Use "we" for analysis ("we tested", "we found"), "you" for recommendations.
- Avoid superlatives that can't be backed by data ("the absolute best").

### CTA Patterns (in order of frequency)

1. **Download / Play Now** button — embedded in each app section
2. **"Claim Your Bonus"** — end of each app review card
3. **FAQ anchor** — "Still have questions? See our FAQ below."
4. **Internal link** — "Read our full [App Name] review"

## Chapter 5: Forbidden Zone

Do NOT include these — they hurt quality and/or rankings:

1. ❌ **Explaining how to play rummy** — readers already know; this signals low intent-match.
2. ❌ **Starting the article with "Rummy is a popular card game…"** — 100% of thin content opens this way. Skip it.
3. ❌ **Listing app features without ranking or recommending** — readers want a verdict, not a spec sheet.
4. ❌ **Legal disclaimers in the intro** — kills trust immediately. Move to footer or FAQ.
5. ❌ **Fake user reviews** — readers have seen them; they reduce credibility.
6. ❌ **Missing comparison table** — 9/10 top pages have one; its absence is a ranking signal gap.
7. ❌ **Word count < 1200** — insufficient depth signal for this keyword's SERP.
8. ❌ **Generic bonus claims without specific numbers** — "great bonuses" is worthless; "₹5,000 welcome bonus" ranks.

## Chapter 8: Limitations & Self-Check

### What This Data Cannot Tell You

- **Actual ranking factors**: This Skill reflects correlation (what top pages do),
  not causation (why Google ranks them). Technical SEO, backlinks, and domain
  authority are not captured.
- **Content freshness effect**: Bonus amounts, app ratings, and legal status
  change frequently. Verify all specific numbers before publishing.
- **Regional intent variation**: "Best rummy app" in delhi vs. karnataka may
  have different intent signals not captured in a single SERP snapshot.
- **Confidence**: Based on ~20 pages crawled at one point in time. SERP shifts
  quarterly; re-run the distiller every 3–6 months.

### Self-Check Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] Article is 1500–2200 words
- [ ] Comparison table is present
- [ ] Every app mentioned has a specific bonus amount (not generic "great bonuses")
- [ ] "Last updated" date is in the article header
- [ ] FAQ section addresses "is it legal" and "how to withdraw"
- [ ] No disclaimers or legal text in the first 200 words
- [ ] Mobile preview tested (no horizontal scroll, table readable on phone)
- [ ] All bonus amounts and app store ratings verified as of publication date
